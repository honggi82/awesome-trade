Stock Investment and AI Trading Research from 2000 to 2026: A Metadata-Driven Citation Map

Generated: 2026-06-26 · Dataset: 2,700 selected papers

Abstract

This draft review maps stock investment and AI-driven trading research from 2000 through 2026, investigating up to 1,000 candidate papers per year from free public Semantic Scholar metadata and selecting the top 100 papers from each year by citation count (2,700 papers selected).

1. Scope and Methods

Queries covered stock prediction, asset pricing, portfolio optimization, financial time series, machine learning, deep learning, reinforcement learning trading, sentiment/news, market microstructure, high-frequency trading, volatility, and risk. Records were filtered by publication year, screened for explicit stock-investment or AI-trading relevance, deduplicated, and ranked by citation count.

2. Key Findings

  • The 2,700 selected papers account for 982,704 citations in the selected set, with the largest citation mass in 2001.
  • The largest category is General Finance, Surveys, and Trading Systems (776 papers).
  • AI-based stock-investment work broadens the signal surface, but economic value still depends on costs, turnover, capacity, and risk.
  • Papers from 2026 are structurally citation-disadvantaged because citation accumulation is still immature.

7. Limitations

This is a metadata-driven citation map rather than a full systematic review of every PDF. Citation count is an influence signal, not investment advice or evidence of live trading profitability.

8. Conclusion

Stock-investment research increasingly combines asset-pricing and portfolio foundations with machine learning, deep learning, alternative data, and sequential trading decisions.
